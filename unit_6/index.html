The Masters

Sri Swami Vishnu-devanandaji Maharaj (1927-1993) Disciple of Swami Sivananda, following his orders he left India in 1957 to come to the West to spread the teachings of Yoga and his Master. It has been a fundamental factor in the knowledge and expansion of Yoga science in the West. He founded centers and ashrams in many countries and dedicated his whole life to the teaching of Yoga and to the training of teachers who continued their work. Everything he did, he always did in the name of his Master.

H H. Sri Swami Sivanandaji Maharaj (1887-1963) One of the greatest spiritual masters of the twentieth century. He spent most of his life in the village of Rishikesh in northern India. He pursued his career as a physician until he decided to renounce worldly life to follow the spiritual path. He wrote more than 200 books on Yoga and spirituality that continue to guide and inspire thousands of disciples around the world. His motto: Serve, love, give, purify, meditate, perform.
